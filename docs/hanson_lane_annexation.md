# Hanson Lane (Annie Street) Annexation — Reference Brief

Application 25775 | Email: comments@bozeman.net | Subject must reference "Hanson Lane App 25775 Annexation and Zoning"

## What Is Being Proposed

- Annexation of an unincorporated parcel between Oak and Durston along the Fowler Avenue corridor
- Rezoning to R-B (mixed-use residential): 45-foot height max, up to 5 stories
- Extension of Annie Street as a through street into the Harvest Creek neighborhood
- Total developable area: ~5 acres or less once right-of-way is built

## Why R-B Zoning Is Wrong — R-A Is Appropriate

**Surrounding land use is single-family residential.** Neighboring properties are single household residential (R-1/R-2). Cottage Park Lane: single-family homes. North side of Annie/Hanson: single-story duplexes (8 units). Predominant density is ≤6 units/acre, matching R-A classification.

**Spot zoning.** Shoe-horning R-B density into an established low-density area is textbook spot zoning, which is disfavored and unlawful under Montana law (Greater Yellowstone Coalition v. Board of County Com'rs, 2001 MT 99).

**Urban Neighborhood does not require R-B.** The Community Plan's Urban Neighborhood designation is a growth policy statement, not a regulation. It allows "urban density homes in a variety of types, shapes, sizes, and intensities" — R-A satisfies this. The designation applies broadly across the entire city and was not designed for small infill islands surrounded by existing development.

**Lacks transit and services.** R-B zoning is appropriate for areas with good transit, shopping, and community services. This area's closest shopping is over a mile east (30-minute walk each way). No reliable public transit. Only qualifying factor: proximity to a park.

## Precedent Risk

If R-B is approved here, it sets the stage for R-B zoning on ALL parcels along Fowler between Durston and Oak — transforming the entire corridor into high-density development adjacent to single-family neighborhoods.

## Traffic and Safety Concerns

- Fowler Avenue Connector joins Annie Street from Harvest Creek to the parcel's western boundary
- Without traffic calming, Annie Street becomes an unobstructed raceway to Emily Dickinson Elementary School
- Any annexation approval must include definitive speed limits and installed traffic calming measures
- City's plan includes a roundabout at Fowler/Annie intersection for calming

## Connection to Fowler Housing Development

The Hanson Lane annexation is NOT part of the City Commission's agreed consensus process for the adjacent Fowler Housing Development. The annexation decision could predetermine zoning outcomes for the broader Fowler corridor, undermining that consensus process.

## What Neighbors Want

R-A zoning (≤6 units/acre) — consistent with surrounding development pattern, the growth policy, and the future land use map. Include the annexation parcel in the existing consensus process rather than approving zoning in isolation.
