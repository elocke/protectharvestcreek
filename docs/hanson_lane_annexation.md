# Hanson Lane (Annie Street) Annexation — Reference Brief

Application 25775 | Email: comments@bozeman.net | Subject must reference "Hanson Lane App 25775 Annexation and Zoning"

## The Three Projects on the 20-Acre Field West of New Holland Drive

The Hanson Lane vote does not stand alone. Three projects are planned on the open field west of New Holland Drive:

- **Fowler Housing Development** — Phase 2 of the consensus engagement process between the City and Harvest Creek HOA begins mid-May 2026, currently including only four known Harvest Creek homeowners.
- **Fowler Avenue Connector** — three segments, each with its own budget. The northern segment from Oak Street to Durston Road is at 90% design and construction is expected to start summer 2026.
- **Hanson Lane Annexation & Zoning** — 10.03-acre parcel acquired as part of the Connector. The portion not used for the road is the subject of Application #25-775. The landowner is seeking R-B; Harvest Creek is asking for R-A.

## Where Things Stand (Live as of May 2026)

- **April 20, 2026** — Community Development Board hearing. Despite over 84 written public comments and 12 oral public comments opposing R-B, the CDB recommended approval to the City Commission.
- **Tuesday, May 5, 2026** — City Commission votes back-to-back on the annexation and the zoning. Agenda item N. Meeting at 6:00 PM, City Hall, 121 N Rouse Ave.
- **Comment deadline** — by not later than noon on Tuesday, May 5, 2026, sent to comments@bozeman.net with subject "Hanson Lane App 25775 Annexation and Zoning". In-person and remote oral comments are also taken at the meeting.

## What Is Being Proposed

- Annexation of an unincorporated parcel between Oak and Durston along the Fowler Avenue corridor
- Rezoning to R-B (mixed-use residential): 45-foot height max, up to 5 stories
- Extension of Annie Street as a through street, connecting Harvest Creek east-to-west into the Oak Springs Subdivision
- Total developable area: ~5 acres or less once the right-of-way is built

## Why R-B Zoning Is Wrong — R-A Is Appropriate

**Surrounding land use is single-family residential.** Neighboring properties are single household residential (R-1/R-2). Cottage Park Lane: single-family homes. North side of Annie/Hanson: single-story duplexes (8 units). Predominant density is ≤6 units/acre, matching R-A classification. No buildings in the area of the subject property are higher than 2 stories.

**Spot zoning.** Shoe-horning R-B density into an established low-density area is textbook spot zoning, which is disfavored and unlawful under Montana law (Greater Yellowstone Coalition v. Board of County Com'rs, 2001 MT 99).

**The new UDC 2025 distinguishes R-A from R-B clearly.** R-A combines the attributes of the former R-1 and R-2 zones: single-family through two-family residences, one and two stories, capped at 40 feet. R-B as requested by the applicant would allow five 5-story buildings at 8 dwelling units per acre as of right.

**Urban Neighborhood does not require R-B.** The Community Plan's Urban Neighborhood designation is a growth policy statement, not a regulation. The Urban Neighborhood designation has the greatest variety of assigned zoning districts in the BCP — seven different zoning districts. Goal N.1.11 of the BCP 2025 calls for "gradual and predictable density in developed areas over time." Under the new BCP, R-A "is designed to hold multiple types of housing on a variety of lot sizes." R-A satisfies this designation. R-B will not produce gradual or predictable increase in density given the surrounding R-1 and R-2 build-out.

**Lacks transit and services.** R-B zoning is appropriate for areas with good transit, shopping, and community services. The closest shopping is over a mile east — a 30-minute walk each way. No reliable public transit. Only qualifying factor: proximity to a park.

**The Fischer/Winn pledge.** On April 14, 2026, in open session of the Bozeman City Commission, Deputy Mayor Douglas Fischer and City Manager Chuck Winn pledged that no 4- or 5-story development will occur on the City-owned parcel immediately adjacent to the subject property. The development challenges for both parcels are the same. The outcomes should be the same.

## Precedent Risk

If R-B is approved here, it sets the stage for R-B zoning on every parcel along Fowler between Durston and Oak — transforming the entire corridor into high-density development adjacent to single-family neighborhoods.

## Annie Street and Emily Dickinson Elementary

The annexation is needed to install the Fowler Avenue right-of-way. The Connector will join Annie Street from Harvest Creek to its dead-end on the western boundary of the subject property and connect through to the Oak Springs Subdivision. The City's plan describes a roundabout at Fowler and Annie.

Without traffic calming, Annie Street becomes an unobstructed raceway to Emily Dickinson Elementary School at 2435 Annie Street. Children walk this route daily. Recent local experience shows crosswalks paired with roundabouts are a problem. Any annexation approval must include definitive speed limits and installed traffic calming, with the impact on young pedestrians evaluated before the decision.

## Connection to Fowler Housing Development

The Hanson Lane annexation is NOT part of the City Commission's agreed consensus process for the adjacent Fowler Housing Development. Phase 2 of that consensus process begins mid-May. Approving R-B on Hanson Lane before the consensus process produces results would predetermine zoning outcomes for the broader Fowler corridor and undermine the process the Commission committed to.

## Volume and Pattern of Public Comment

More than 90 public comments have already been submitted on this matter. The CDB recommended approval anyway. The volume of comment, combined with significant community impact already changing the design of the Fowler Connection between Durston and Oak, demonstrates that the surrounding neighborhood treats this as residential, not as the kind of mixed-use district R-B is designed for. All community input on the road design assumed those lands would remain zoned SRR or at least low-density residential.

## What Neighbors Want

R-A zoning (≤6 units/acre, ≤40 feet, ≤2 stories) — consistent with surrounding development pattern, the growth policy, the future land use map, and Goal N.1.11. Include the annexation parcel in the existing consensus process rather than approving zoning in isolation. Definitive traffic calming on Annie Street as a condition of any annexation. Speed limits, installed calming measures, and pedestrian-impact study before the vote.
